Pythagorean Theorem:

In a right triangle, the square of the length of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the lengths of the other two sides.
Formula: \( a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \)

Fundamental Theorem of Calculus:

If a function is continuous on a closed interval [a, b] and has an antiderivative, then the integral of the function over that interval is equal to the difference of the values of the antiderivative at the endpoints.
Formula: \( \int_a^b f(x)\, dx = F(b) - F(a) \)

Euler's Formula:

For any real number \( \theta \), the following identity holds:
Formula: \( e^{i\theta} = \cos(\theta) + i\sin(\theta) \)

Binomial Theorem:

The expansion of \( (x + y)^n \) is given by the sum of terms of the form \( \binom{n}{k}x^{n-k}y^k \), where \( \binom{n}{k} \) is a binomial coefficient.
Formula: \( (x + y)^n = \sum_{k=0}^{n} \binom{n}{k}x^{n-k}y^k \)

Law of Sines:

In any triangle, the ratio of the length of a side to the sine of its opposite angle is constant.
Formula: \( \frac{a}{\sin(A)} = \frac{b}{\sin(B)} = \frac{c}{\sin(C)} \)
